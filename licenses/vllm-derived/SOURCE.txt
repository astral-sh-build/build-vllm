Component: MIT- and BSD-licensed source material in vLLM
Project: vLLM
Source: https://github.com/vllm-project/vllm

vLLM is primarily Apache-2.0, but the source tree also contains MIT- and
BSD-licensed material that may be distributed in, or compiled into, the wheel.
Known examples in the vLLM v0.25.0 source include:

- `csrc/cpu/float_convert.hpp`, which includes MIT-licensed code copied from
  PrincetonVision/marvin.
- `vllm/model_executor/models/phi.py`, which retains MIT notice material from
  Microsoft Phi code and BSD-3-Clause notice material from Tri Dao.
- `vllm/model_executor/layers/fla/`, which includes MIT-licensed material from
  Songlin Yang and Yu Zhang.
- `vllm/model_executor/layers/mamba/ops/`, which includes BSD-3-Clause notice
  material from Tri Dao and Albert Gu.

Python source files shipped in the wheel retain their inline notices. This
managed notice file is included so the wheel also documents the non-Apache
licenses in its `.dist-info/licenses` directory.
